Warren Edward Buffett
August 30, 1930 (age 92)


Warren Buffett-He is one of the best known fundamental investors in the world.

Early Life and Education: Warren Buffett was born on August 30, 1930, in Omaha, Nebraska. He displayed an interest in business and investing from a young age, delivering newspapers and investing in stocks during his teenage years. He graduated from the University of Nebraska and went on to study at Columbia Business School under Benjamin Graham, a renowned value investor.

Investment Philosophy: Buffett is known for his value investing approach. He seeks undervalued companies with strong fundamentals and long-term growth potential. His philosophy revolves around buying businesses he understands, holding them for the long term, and not being swayed by short-term market fluctuations.

Berkshire Hathaway: In 1962, Buffett began buying shares of a struggling textile manufacturing company called Berkshire Hathaway. Over time, he transformed it into a diversified conglomerate and used its cash flows to invest in other businesses and stocks. Today, Berkshire Hathaway is known for its vast portfolio of companies, including Geico, Dairy Queen, and Coca-Cola.

Long-Term Track Record: Buffett's success as an investor is demonstrated by his long-term track record. He consistently outperformed the market and achieved remarkable returns for Berkshire Hathaway shareholders over several decades. His ability to generate compounding returns has made him one of the wealthiest individuals in the world.

Philanthropy and Giving Pledge: Despite his immense wealth, Buffett has remained humble and committed to giving back. In 2006, he pledged to donate a significant portion of his wealth to philanthropic causes. He is a vocal supporter of the Giving Pledge, encouraging other billionaires to commit the majority of their fortunes to charitable endeavors.

Mentorship and Influence: Buffett has mentored and influenced many successful investors and business leaders. His annual letters to Berkshire Hathaway shareholders are widely read and valued for their insights into investing and business management. He has also spoken extensively on various platforms and has become a trusted voice in the financial world.

Personal Traits: Buffett is known for his simplicity and frugality, despite his immense wealth. He lives in the same house he bought in 1958 and is often seen enjoying a cheeseburger and a cherry Coke. His down-to-earth personality and approachability have endeared him to many people.

Quotes and Wisdom: Buffett is famous for his memorable quotes and investment wisdom. His quotes often reflect his straightforward and rational approach to investing, such as "Rule No. 1: Never lose money. Rule No. 2: Never forget Rule No. 1" and "Price is what you pay. Value is what you get.
